QR Codes for Accountants

Practical QR code ideas for accountants, including where to place codes, what to link to and how to avoid scanning problems.

A practical accountants QR workflow

Accounting practices can use QR codes to reduce back-and-forth around appointments, document requests and review collection. The clearest results come from linking each code to one action rather than a general home page.

Useful QR links for Accountants

  • Appointment booking and consultation request form
  • Secure document upload link for tax packs or payroll files
  • Client portal, contact page and opening-hours link
  • Google review request after a completed return or audit
  • PDF checklist for annual deadlines and reminders
  • Practice brochure or service overview for new clients

Where to place the code

  • Reception desk: contact page and appointment form
  • Invoice emails: document upload or payment reminder link
  • Client welcome packs: annual checklist and service overview
  • Window or sign: contact details for walk-in enquiries
  • Email confirmation: review request and portal reminder

Recommended QR types

Use a static QR code for a permanent contact page, PDF checklist or office map. Switch to a dynamic or tracked link when you want to change the destination later or measure which service gets the most scans.

Common mistakes and practical checks

  • Avoid sending people to a login page without a clear landing page for first-time visitors
  • Do not bury the action in a long URL or a generic homepage
  • Test the code from a phone at the same angle and lighting your clients will use
  • Keep the label short and specific, such as “Scan for invoice help”
