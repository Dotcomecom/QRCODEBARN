QR Codes for Garages

Practical QR code ideas for garages, including where to place codes, what to link to and how to avoid scanning problems.

Ad space

A practical garages QR workflow

Garages can use QR codes to speed up bookings, explain services and collect reviews at the moment a customer is most likely to act. The code should point to the next useful step rather than a broad home page.

Useful QR links for Garages

  • Service booking form for MOTs, repairs and diagnostics
  • Vehicle servicing checklist or service history page
  • Warranty or recall information page
  • Google review request after a completed job
  • Location and opening-hours page
  • Parts or tyre enquiry form

Where to place the code

  • Reception desk: booking and contact page
  • Workshop noticeboard: service checklist and updates
  • Invoice: review request and follow-up appointment
  • Window display: opening hours and service list
  • Brochure: tyre and MOT information

Recommended QR types

Use a static QR code for a fixed service page or contact page. Choose a dynamic QR code when you rotate seasonal offers, track which services are most popular or change the destination later.

Common mistakes and practical checks

  • Avoid codes placed on oily or dirty surfaces that can reduce scan quality
  • Do not use one code for every service without a clear label
  • Test the code in the workshop environment and the front office
  • Make the action label obvious, such as “Scan for booking”
