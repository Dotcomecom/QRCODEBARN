QR Codes for Electricians

Practical QR code ideas for electricians, including where to place codes, what to link to and how to avoid scanning problems.

Ad space

A practical electricians QR workflow

Electricians can use QR codes to put emergency contact details, service booking and job references in front of customers without needing a long explanation. A small code on a van, sign or invoice can be surprisingly useful.

Useful QR links for Electricians

  • Emergency call-out booking form
  • Service checklist or quote request page
  • Reviews and testimonials for recent jobs
  • Location and coverage area map
  • Safety tips or aftercare advice for jobs
  • Social media and company profile link

Where to place the code

  • Van door or side panel: call-out booking and service area
  • Invoice or quote: review request and contact details
  • Office sign: emergency contact and business profile
  • Brochure or flyer: quote request and service list
  • Job completion sheet: aftercare guidance and feedback link

Recommended QR types

Use a static QR code for a stable contact page or service page. A dynamic or tracked QR code is better when you want to change the destination for different campaigns or track which advert performs best.

Common mistakes and practical checks

  • Avoid placing the code where dirt, vehicle paint or glare reduce scan quality
  • Do not direct customers to a generic homepage when they need a booking form
  • Test it on a phone outdoors and next to the vehicle before final installation
  • Keep the label simple such as “Scan to book”
