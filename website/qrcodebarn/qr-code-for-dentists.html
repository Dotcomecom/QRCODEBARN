QR Codes for Dentists

Practical QR code ideas for dentists, including where to place codes, what to link to and how to avoid scanning problems.

Ad space

A practical dentists QR workflow

Dental practices can use QR codes to guide patients to booking, directions and preventative advice. People often scan while they are already in the waiting room, so the code should give immediate value.

Useful QR links for Dentists

  • Appointment booking or treatment enquiry form
  • Directions, parking and opening-hours page
  • Patient aftercare instructions and follow-up notes
  • Google review request after a visit
  • New patient registration or insurance form
  • Social contact page for clinic updates

Where to place the code

  • Reception desk: booking and review request
  • Waiting room: aftercare information and FAQs
  • Front door: directions and parking information
  • Practice cards: new patient registration link
  • Email confirmation: follow-up guidance and contact details

Recommended QR types

A static QR code works well for a stable contact or directions page. Use a dynamic or tracked QR code if you want to change the destination later or monitor which message gets the most scans.

Common mistakes and practical checks

  • Avoid putting the code on a wall where it is too high or too low to scan comfortably
  • Do not send patients to a generic homepage when a booking page exists
  • Test from a typical phone position before printing the final sign
  • Make the code label match the patient need, such as “Scan for directions”
