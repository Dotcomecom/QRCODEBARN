Best restaurant QR code uses

The strongest restaurant QR codes solve a specific customer action. Avoid one vague QR code that tries to do everything. Create separate QR codes for menus, booking links, review requests and WiFi access so each placement has a clear purpose.

Menus

Put digital menus on tables, windows and hotel rooms.

Reviews

Send happy guests directly to your review page.

WiFi

Let guests connect without typing passwords.

Events

Promote quiz nights, live music and seasonal offers.

Where to place restaurant QR codes

Place QR codes where the customer is already making a decision: table cards for menus, receipts for reviews, windows for bookings, and flyers for events. Always add a short instruction beside the code.

Restaurant QR tracking and testing

For basic use, a static QR code is enough. For campaigns, use a trackable URL or analytics-tagged link before generating the QR code. Test the final printed version in normal lighting before using it with customers.

Frequently asked questions

What QR codes should a restaurant use?
The most useful QR codes are menu QR codes, booking QR codes, review QR codes, WiFi QR codes and special offer QR codes.
Can a restaurant use several QR codes?
Yes. Separate QR codes for separate actions are clearer for customers and easier to measure.
Where should restaurants display QR codes?
Common placements include table cards, receipts, windows, menus, flyers, takeaway bags and social media graphics.
Do QR codes replace printed menus?
Not necessarily. Many restaurants use QR menus alongside printed menus to give guests both options.
