Wedding QR code ideas

Wedding QR codes are ideal for information that changes or would make an invitation crowded. Link to an RSVP form, a wedding website, a venue map, gift list, menu choices, accommodation notes or a photo upload page.

Design tips for invitation QR codes

Keep the QR code clean, high contrast and surrounded by enough white space. Add a simple label such as “Scan to RSVP” or “Scan for guest details”. Avoid placing the code over busy patterns.

Make it easy for guests

The destination page should open quickly and show the most important action first. For RSVP forms, keep the form short and mobile-friendly.

Frequently asked questions

Can I put a QR code on a wedding invitation?
Yes. Many couples use QR codes for RSVPs, venue directions and wedding websites.
What should a wedding QR code link to?
Common options include RSVP forms, venue maps, guest information pages, gift lists and wedding websites.
Will older guests be able to use it?
Most modern phone cameras scan QR codes, but it is sensible to include a short written URL as a backup.
Can I update the wedding information later?
Yes, if the QR code links to a webpage you control, you can update that page after invitations have been sent.
