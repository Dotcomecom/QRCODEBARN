Event QR code ideas

Event QR codes work best when they remove friction. Use them on posters, badges, invitations, table plans, stage signage and follow-up emails. Each QR code should lead to a page designed for phones.

  • Ticket booking pages
  • Registration forms
  • Venue maps and parking details
  • Conference schedules
  • Wedding RSVPs
  • Feedback surveys

Before, during and after the event

Before the event, QR codes can drive registration. During the event, they can help with navigation, check-in and schedules. After the event, they can collect feedback, share photo galleries or promote the next event.

How to print event QR codes

Large venue posters need larger QR codes than invitations or badges. Test scanning at the real distance. A QR code on a wall poster may need to be much larger than one on a printed programme.

Frequently asked questions

What can an event QR code link to?
It can link to a ticket page, registration form, map, timetable, app download, livestream, photo gallery or feedback form.
Can I use QR codes for wedding RSVPs?
Yes. Link the QR code to an RSVP form, wedding website or guest information page.
How big should an event QR code be?
It depends on distance. Small printed materials can use smaller codes, but wall signs and posters need larger codes.
Should I test an event QR code before printing?
Yes. Always test the final QR code on multiple phones before printing in bulk.
