How to create a QR code menu

Upload your menu to your website, Google Drive, Dropbox, PDF host or online ordering system, then paste the public menu link into the generator. Once the QR code is created, test it on at least two phones before printing it for tables, windows or takeaway bags.

  1. Prepare a mobile-friendly menu page or PDF.
  2. Paste the menu link into the generator.
  3. Generate and save the QR code.
  4. Add it to table talkers, posters, flyers or receipts.
  5. Update the online menu whenever prices or dishes change.

Best practice for QR menus

A good QR menu should load quickly, display clearly on mobile and avoid tiny PDF text. Use clear labels such as “Scan for Menu”, place the QR code where customers naturally look, and include a short fallback URL for guests who prefer typing.

Printing menu QR codes

Print large enough for easy scanning. For table cards, a QR code around 3–4 cm wide usually works well. For windows or wall posters, increase the size and test at the expected scanning distance. Keep enough white space around the code so phone cameras can read it accurately.

Frequently asked questions

Can I make a free QR code for a restaurant menu?
Yes. Paste the public link to your menu page or PDF into the generator and create a scannable QR code.
Can I update the menu after printing the QR code?
If the QR code points to a webpage or PDF URL that you control, you can update the content at that URL without reprinting the code.
Should a menu QR code link to a PDF or webpage?
A mobile webpage is usually best, but a well-formatted PDF can also work if it loads quickly and is easy to read on a phone.
Do customers need an app to scan a menu QR code?
Most modern phone cameras scan QR codes directly without a separate app.
