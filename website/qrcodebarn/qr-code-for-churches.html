QR Codes for Churches

Practical QR code ideas for churches, including where to place codes, what to link to and how to avoid scanning problems.

A practical churches QR workflow

Churches can use QR codes to connect people to services, notices and contact details quickly. The most useful codes are those that help visitors find the next step without asking for a long explanation.

Useful QR links for Churches

  • Weekly service times and location map
  • Prayer request or contact form
  • Children’s or youth group sign-up
  • Event poster with service details and parking
  • Donation page for a specific appeal
  • Social pages for updates and live streams

Where to place the code

  • Entrance sign: service times and map
  • Noticeboard: event poster and contact form
  • Bulletin inserts: donation or sign-up link
  • Community hall: WiFi page and events
  • Email newsletters: latest service link

Recommended QR types

A static QR code is ideal for a stable service page or contact page. Use a dynamic QR code when you want to swap posters seasonally or track which events generate the most interest.

Common mistakes and practical checks

  • Avoid placing codes too low on signs where children or people in wheelchairs may not see them
  • Do not link to a general homepage when a detailed event page exists
  • Test the code in daylight and at night if the sign is illuminated
  • Keep the label simple such as “Scan for service times”
