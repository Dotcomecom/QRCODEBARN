QR Codes for Holiday Cottages

Practical QR code ideas for holiday cottages, including where to place codes, what to link to and how to avoid scanning problems.

Ad space

A practical holiday cottages QR workflow

Holiday cottage hosts can use QR codes to make arrival instructions, local recommendations and property details easy to access during the stay. Guests often scan at the front door, kitchen table or welcome pack.

Useful QR links for Holiday Cottages

  • Arrival instructions and key access details
  • WiFi and house manual page
  • Local attraction and restaurant guide
  • Review request after the stay
  • Parking and contact instructions
  • Booking or rebooking enquiry page

Where to place the code

  • Front door: arrival and WiFi link
  • Welcome pack: house rules and local recommendations
  • Kitchen noticeboard: contact and parking guide
  • Email confirmation: review and next-stay link
  • Property sign: access instructions and key details

Recommended QR types

Use a static QR code for a permanent WiFi page or property guide. A dynamic QR code works better if you want to update local recommendations seasonally or send different messages to different guests.

Common mistakes and practical checks

  • Avoid codes that are hidden inside a book or low-contrast welcome pack
  • Do not send guests to a generic page when a direct arrival guide exists
  • Test the code at the entrance and on the kitchen table
  • Make the action label clear such as “Scan for WiFi”
