QR Codes for Farm Shops

Practical QR code ideas for farm shops, including where to place codes, what to link to and how to avoid scanning problems.

Ad space

A practical farm shops QR workflow

Farm shops can use QR codes to connect customers to opening hours, local produce stories and online orders without adding friction. The code should feel useful at the moment the customer is already browsing the shelves or a sign.

Useful QR links for Farm Shops

  • Seasonal produce list and daily availability
  • Online shop or click-and-collect page
  • Event or market day calendar
  • Google review request after a visit
  • Story page about the farm, suppliers and recipes
  • Directions and parking details for visitors

Where to place the code

  • Entrance sign: opening hours and directions
  • Produce displays: product stories and online shop
  • Counter: review request and loyalty sign-up
  • Events board: market dates and seasonal offers
  • Receipts: follow-up offer or review link

Recommended QR types

Use a static QR code for a permanent opening-hours or directions page. Use a dynamic or tracked link when you rotate offers, run events or want to update the destination after printing.

Common mistakes and practical checks

  • Avoid putting the code on a busy display where it competes with product labels
  • Do not send customers to a generic homepage when a local page exists
  • Test the code in daylight and in the shade of the shop
  • Keep the text short and descriptive
