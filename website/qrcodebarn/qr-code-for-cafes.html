QR Codes for Cafes

Practical QR code ideas for cafes, including where to place codes, what to link to and how to avoid scanning problems.

Ad space

A practical cafes QR workflow

Cafes can use QR codes to make ordering, menu access and location details faster for people who are already on the move. The right code should answer one question clearly, such as “where is the menu?” or “how do I book?”

Useful QR links for Cafes

  • Table menu with allergen information and daily specials
  • Ordering page for takeaway or collection
  • Booking link for private hire or group bookings
  • Google review request after a visit
  • WiFi access page for guests
  • Instagram or Facebook page for events and opening hours

Where to place the code

  • Tables: menu and allergen information
  • Counter: collection ordering and pick-up instructions
  • Window: opening hours, location and social media
  • Outdoor signage: wifi access and event updates
  • Receipts or loyalty cards: review request and offers

Recommended QR types

Use a static QR code for a fixed menu or contact page. A dynamic or tracked QR code is better when you want to swap offers seasonally, change the menu destination or measure table scans.

Common mistakes and practical checks

  • Do not place menu codes in low light or behind glass that causes glare
  • Avoid linking to a PDF that is too large to open quickly on mobile
  • Check that your menu text is readable on a phone before printing
  • Make sure the code has a short instruction such as “Scan for menu”
