QR Code Best Practices

QR codes fail most often because they are too small, too crowded, badly contrasted or placed where people cannot comfortably scan them. A good QR code needs enough physical size, quiet space around the edge, strong contrast and a clear call to action.

Use plain language near the code. A label such as Scan for menu, Scan for WiFi, Scan to book or Scan for directions tells people exactly why they should use it.

Always test from realistic scanning distances. A QR code on a table menu can be smaller than a QR code on a shop window, poster or roadside board.
