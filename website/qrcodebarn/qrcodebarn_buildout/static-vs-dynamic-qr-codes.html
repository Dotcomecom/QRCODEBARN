Static vs Dynamic QR Codes

A static QR code stores the final destination directly inside the code. Once printed, the embedded content cannot be changed. Static QR codes are suitable for fixed information such as a permanent website address or evergreen contact page.

A dynamic QR code usually points first to a short redirect URL. The final destination can then be changed later. Dynamic QR codes are useful for campaigns, menus, seasonal offers and printed material that may need updating.

The trade-off is simple: static QR codes are direct and durable; dynamic QR codes are flexible but depend on the redirect service continuing to work.
